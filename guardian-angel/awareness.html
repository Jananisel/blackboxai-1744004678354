Safety Awareness

Empowering you with knowledge and resources to stay safe in various situations

Street Safety

  • Walk confidently and be aware of your surroundings
  • Keep valuables concealed and bags zipped
  • Plan your route in advance and stick to well-lit areas

Public Transport

  • Wait in designated, well-lit areas
  • Have your ticket/pass ready to avoid delays
  • Sit near the driver or in busy carriages

Night Safety

  • Share your location with trusted contacts
  • Pre-book licensed taxis or use ride-sharing apps
  • Avoid walking alone if possible

Online Privacy

  • Review privacy settings on social media
  • Be cautious about sharing personal information
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account

Cyber Stalking

  • Document all unwanted communications
  • Block and report offenders on platforms
  • Consider changing usernames/emails if needed

Scam Prevention

  • Verify unexpected requests for money/info
  • Look for HTTPS and padlock icons on websites
  • Be wary of too-good-to-be-true offers

Taxi Safety

  • Check license plate matches app details
  • Share ride details with trusted contacts
  • Sit in the back seat for personal space

Hotel Safety

  • Request rooms near elevators, not ground floor
  • Use all door locks and security features
  • Keep valuables in room safe when possible

Public Places

  • Be aware of emergency exits in new locations
  • Keep drinks in sight at all times
  • Trust your instincts about people/situations

Workplace Rights

  • You have the right to a harassment-free workplace
  • Document incidents with dates/times/witnesses
  • Report to HR or appropriate authorities

Domestic Violence

  • You have legal protection under PWDVA 2005
  • Can file for protection orders from courts
  • Access to shelters and support services

Cyber Laws

  • IT Act Section 66E protects privacy violations
  • Section 67 covers obscene content sharing
  • Can file complaints with cyber crime cells
